Photo Culling App for Mac

Proof is built for the first serious review pass: move through a shoot quickly, mark what stays, reject what misses, compare close frames, and keep the original files untouched.

Download for Mac Read the workflow guide

Cull large photo shoots without building a cloud library

Proof works with folders where they already live on your Mac. Add a folder, let Proof index thumbnails and metadata, then review the shoot with fast flags, star ratings, color labels, filters, and keyboard navigation.

The app is local-first by design. Your originals stay on disk, your catalog stays on your Mac, and exports are written as new files instead of changing the source images.

Built around the culling pass

Pick and reject quickly

Use P for picks, X for rejects, 0-5 for ratings, and 6-9 for color labels.

Compare similar frames

Use Survey to compare selected photos side by side when timing, focus, expression, or composition are close.

Inspect in Loupe

Open a single photo large, zoom in, pan around, and check detail before deciding what stays in the set.

Export finished copies

Export selected photos to JPEG, PNG, TIFF, or WebP while keeping the source files unchanged.

Where Proof fits

Proof is not trying to replace every editing tool. It focuses on the review stage before deeper editing: folders, thumbnails, metadata, selection, comparison, cleanup, and export. That makes it useful for photographers who want a calm Mac-native culling app before handing selects to another editor.

If you already organize shoots by folder, Proof keeps that workflow intact. If you are moving away from a heavy cloud-first library, Proof gives you a local catalog without moving your image archive into a new service.
