Develop

Develop tools are being refined for a future release.

The current release focuses on fast culling and local library work. The editing surface is being shaped as a calmer, non-destructive place for future photo adjustments without changing the core workflow.

What is being refined

  • Exposure and color controls for future edits
  • Fuji X Raw workflow - render JPGs from your camera's engine
  • Local, non-destructive editing workflow

What stays true

Proof keeps the same quiet, local-first approach: files stay on disk, the catalog stays local, and the app stays focused on photographers who want a fast review pass before deeper work.

Workflow

A workflow that stays out of the way

Proof fits into the way photographers already work: files on disk, folders by shoot, quick passes for picks and rejects, deeper inspection for close calls, then hand off to the editor you already use.

01

Add a folder

Choose a folder of photos. Proof indexes metadata, creates thumbnails, and keeps the originals in place.

02

Cull fast

Navigate with the keyboard. Press `P` for picks, `X` for rejects, `0-5` for ratings, and `6-9` for color labels.

03

Compare and refine

Use Survey to compare selected frames or Loupe to inspect one image at a time.

04

Organize selects

Move files to folders, create subfolders, add photos to collections, set keywords, and filter by metadata.

Who it is for

For serious hobbyists and photo enthusiasts

Proof is currently best for photographers who want a fast, local way to review, compare, and organize personal shoots without committing to a heavy cloud workflow.

Serious hobbyists

Cull larger personal shoots, compare similar frames, and keep your best images moving.

Photo enthusiasts

Stay close to your files, ratings, labels, and local folders without a cloud-first library.

Travel and family photographers

Work through trips, events, and everyday shoots with a focused review pass.

Growing toward commercial trust

Proof aims to add the robustness needed for professional and commercial photography workflows over time.
